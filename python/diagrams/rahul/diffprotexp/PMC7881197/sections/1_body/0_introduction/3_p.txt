0: Arsenic, a naturally forming element that often occurs in combination with sulfur and other metals, is an environmental contaminant found in drinking water 9.
1: The WHO and the International Agency for Research on Cancer categorize inorganic arsenic as carcinogenic, and provisional guidelines recommend a concentration below 10 g/L 10.
2: However, it has been reported that about 50 countries around the world have drinking water with concentrations of inorganic arsenic over the recommended level 11,  12.
3: Exposure to inorganic arsenic over a prolonged period of time is reportedly linked to the occurrence of skin, bladder, and lung cancer 13,  14.
