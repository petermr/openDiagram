0: The insects used in the tests were reared at the Laboratory of Entomology at the Regional Research Center on Horticulture and Organic Agriculture (RRCHOA), University of Sousse, Tunisia.
1: Spodoptera littoralis (Lepidoptera: Noctuidae) was reared on artificial diet consisting of a mixture of wheat germ, beer yeast, maize semolina, ascorbic acid, nipagine, benzoic acid, agar and water (Poitout and Bues, 1970[ 43]) at 26  2 C, with a photoperiod of 16:8 h light: dark (L:D) and 75  5 % relative humidity (RH) in a growth chamber.
2: The third instar larvae of  S. littoralis were used for the test.
3: Tribolium castaneum (Coleoptera: Tenebrionidae) was reared on wheat flour mixed with yeast (10:1, w/w).
4: The cultures were maintained in a growth chamber at 28  2 C, with a RH of 75  5 %, in the dark.
5: Only new emerged adults were used for the test.
