0: Congenital heart defects are the most common birth defects, affecting nearly 40,000 infants each year in the United States of America 1  3.
1: The majority of forms of congenital heart disease (CHD) presenting during the neonatal period require surgery on cardiopulmonary bypass (CPB) in early infancy and multiple subsequent surgeries.
2: Of these, hypoplastic left heart syndrome (HLHS) remains the most critical form of congenital heart disease, requiring as a first stage of surgical palliation the Norwood operation or its modifications, typically performed during the early neonatal period.
3: The Norwood operation is one of the highest risk operations for CHD, with mortality rate as high as 19% 4.
