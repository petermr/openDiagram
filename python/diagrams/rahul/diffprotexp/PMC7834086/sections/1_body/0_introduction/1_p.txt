0: The Pacific white shrimp,  Penaeus vannamei, is one of the most important aquaculture shrimp species globally, accounting for nearly 80% or two-thirds of the total annual produce [ 1].
1: Unfortunately, the aquaculture industry suffers from various diseases and pathogenic infections caused by bacteria and viruses, which results in substantial economic losses [ 2, 3].
2: Among shrimp bacterial diseases, acute hepatopancreatic necrosis disease (AHPND) is the most pathogenic and devastating disease that affects shrimp aquaculture [ 4].
3: Since the first outbreak of AHPND in China in 2009 and its subsequent spread to other countries such as Vietnam, Malaysia, Thailand, the Philippines, Mexico, etc., AHPND has caused billions of dollars in economic losses to farmers [ 57].
