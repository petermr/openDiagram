0: Sulpiride, an antipsychotic drug, is a selective dopamine receptor antagonist.
1: It has high affinity to D2 and D4 receptors.
2: It has been reported that sulpiride has an anti-inflammatory effect through increasing the intracellular cAMP level regardless the presence of dopamine signaling at D2 receptors (Brustolim et al.,  2006).
3: Based on this finding, it can be speculated that the drug can have a beneficial effect in the management of allergic rhinitis inflammatory manifestations.
4: Sulpiride experiences a limited oral bioavailability not more than 27% due to its poor water solubility (Zidan et al.,  2015).
