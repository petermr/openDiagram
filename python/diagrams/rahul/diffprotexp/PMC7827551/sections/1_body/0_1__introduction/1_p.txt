0: Non-tuberculous mycobacteria (NTM) have less information compared to the classical mycobacterial pathogens such as  Mycobacterium tuberculosis ( M. tuberculosis) and  M. leprae.
1: The incidence of NTM diseases shows a dramatic rising over the past several years, especially in developed countries.
2: In the United States, particularly among women and older age groups, the incidence has increased from 3.13 to 4.73 per 100,000 person-years from 2008 to 2015 [ 1].
3: NTM disease has reached 14.7 cases per 100,000 people based on national surveillance data in 2014 in Japan [ 2].
4: The prevalence of NTM lung disease appears to be increasing and is expected to overcome that of  M. tuberculosis in the next few years [ 3].
