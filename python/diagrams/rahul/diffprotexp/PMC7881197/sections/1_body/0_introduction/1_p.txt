0: Cancer is one of the leading causes of human mortality; according to the World Health Organization (WHO), it ranked sixth among the top 10 causes of global death in 2016 ( https://www.who.int/news-room/fact-sheets/detail/the-top-10-causes-of-death).
1: Although a measurable number of cancers are both preventable and treatable, no anticancer drug or method has yet been developed to prevent and treat cancers effectively without affecting normal cells 1.
2: Over many years, researchers worldwide have made extensive and rigorous efforts to identify and develop such drugs.
3: Breast cancer is the most common cancer in women.
4: It is estimated that more than 508,000 women worldwide died from breast cancer in 2011 (WHO,  https://www.who.int/cancer/detection/breastcancer/en/index1.html), and about 42,170 women have died from the disease so far in 2020 in the USA (American Cancer Society,  https://www.cancer.org/cancer/breast-cancer/about/how-common-is-breast-cancer.html).
5: The disease affects both developed and developing countries similarly 2.
6: The survival rates among breast cancer patients with the current remedies are low and vary by country, ranging from 40 to 80% 2.
7: The worldwide incidence has been increasing by 0.3% per year 3.
