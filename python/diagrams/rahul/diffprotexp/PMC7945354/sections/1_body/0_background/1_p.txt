0: Diabetes mellitus is a group of metabolic disorders characterized by chronic hyperglycemia caused by complicated etiologies.
1: Statistical data organized by the International Diabetes Federation revealed that there were approximately 387 million people worldwide who had diabetes mellitus in 2014, which is estimated to increase to 592 million in 2035 [ 1].
2: Diabetes mellitus occurs when the body cannot produce enough insulin or use insulin effectively.
3: The former is defined as type 1 diabetes mellitus (T1DM), and the latter is type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM) [ 2].
4: Increasing evidence has revealed that T2DM is associated not only with microvascular complications (including nephropathy, retinopathy and neuropathy) and macrovascular complications (such as cardiovascular diseases) [ 3] but also with the oncogenesis and development of multiple types of cancer, including lung cancer, breast cancer and pancreatic cancer [ 4,  5].
