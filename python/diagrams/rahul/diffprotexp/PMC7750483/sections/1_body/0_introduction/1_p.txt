0: Gastrointestinal (GI) cancer is one of the leading causes of death worldwide.
1: It refers to cancers of the upper and lower digestive tracts and mainly includes colorectal adenocarcinoma (CRC), gastric cancer (GC), pancreatic cancer (PC), hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC), and esophageal cancer (EC) ( Toomey et al., 2013).
2: Nearly 4.1 million people are diagnosed with GI cancer each year.
3: Epigenetic changes are common events in both initiation and progression of GI cancer ( Vedeld et al., 2018).
4: Currently, there are many ways to treat GI cancer.
5: However, most of the treatment outcomes are still poor ( Bilgin et al., 2017).
