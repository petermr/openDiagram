0: The dental pulp consists of various cell types, each with specified roles.
1: The primary function of the dental pulp is to form dentin via odontoblasts.
2: It also has nutritive, sensory, protective, and other formative functions [ 1].
3: Following dental tissue damage, initiation of an inflammatory response known as pulpitis occurs.
4: After removal of the damage causative factor, the dental pulp will return to normal.
5: However, if damage persists, the pulpitis becomes irreversible and leads to necrosis [ 2].
