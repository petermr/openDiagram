0: Plasmodium falciparum causes the deadliest form of malaria, which impacts over 200 million individuals and results in nearly 450,000 deaths each year, 60% of which are children aged under 5 years [ 1].
1: It belongs to the large Apicomplexa phylum of diverse eukaryotic intracellular parasites.
2: In addition to malaria, this phylum also encompasses infectious agents of cryptosporidiosis and toxoplasmosis [ 2].
3: The latter is caused by  Toxoplasma gondii, which is estimated to infect over 30% of the world population, and hence considered as one of the most successful parasites [ 3].
4: These diseases are not only life-threatening but also widespread and, therefore, represent a serious threat to public health.
